Known issues in the concepts

As the library has no real community or core-developer this part of the documentation is thought as a collection of known problems of higher complexity.

One of the biggest issues is about the general idea how the plotting of axes should be implemented in the drawing-cycle of Gtk+.

Usually the axis-drawing starts with the measurement. As there is in the plain beginning no Tic available on an axis, an automatic routine finds a rather high value for the pixel-difference between different tics.

An adjustment to this high value is a rather high number of possible Tics. Than these tics will be realized and added to the GdvAxis-Container.

After that the Axis is realized and space is allocated for the axis and every Tic. Now the axis measures the free space again and realizes that the space between every tic is much lower than previously thought. So the value for the number of tics is automatically adjusted and the residual Tics will be removed from the axis.

The process starts now with a similar situation as in the beginning and will be continuing with adding, removing and measuring of Tics. This cycle is depicted in the following graphic.

It will be really hard to detect such a cycle and break it from within the code.

For the moment this problem is rather simply solved by measuring adjusting the number of Tics during the axis-allocation by the space needed by the beginning- and ending-Tic, as their values can be easily determined or are even fixed.

Nevertheless, this approach is problematic as in some cases the space needed for the beginning- and ending-tic might differ from the space needed for the tics in between. A simple example might be an axis from 0 to 5e4 with tics at 2.5e4 in between.

However this effect is at the moment seen as the minor problem.
